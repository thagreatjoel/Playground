What is this?

We use electronics every day, but rarely understand what’s inside.

Hardware Playground is a hands-on program where you:

  • open real devices and explore their PCBs
  • build circuits from scratch
  • experiment and see what happens
  • rebuild parts of real electronics

This is about understanding hardware for real — not just using it.

Eligibility

  • You must be 13–18 years old
  • You must be part of the Hack Club Slack community
  • You must not be banned from Hack Club
  • You must verify your identity

Project Requirements

  • Spend at least 1–2 hours per project
  • Build using real hardware (no simulation-only projects)
  • Show proof of work:
    • photos (including build process)
    • demo video
  • Write a short explanation of:
    • what you built
    • how it works
  • Avoid fully prebuilt modules unless you understand them
  • Projects must be new work (not reused old projects)

Rules

  • build real circuits (no simulation-only projects)
  • explain your work in your own words
  • show proof (photos + demo video)
  • spend at least 1–2 hours per project
  • use safe devices and low voltage (≤12V)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this free?

Yes. If you do real work, you’ll earn rewards.

Do I need experience?

No. You can start with simple circuits.

What if I don’t have a device to open?

You can still build circuits and start with beginner projects.

What if my project doesn’t work?

That’s fine. Show what you tried and what you learned.

Can I use AI?

Yes, for learning. But you must understand your work.

Do I need expensive tools?

No. Basic components are enough.

Can I work with friends?

You can discuss ideas, but your work must be your own.

What happens if I copy or fake work?

Your submission will be rejected.
