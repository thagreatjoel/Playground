What is this?

We use electronics every day, but rarely understand what's inside.

Hardware Playground is a hands-on program where you:

  • open real devices and explore their PCBs
  • build circuits from scratch
  • experiment and see what happens
  • rebuild small parts of real electronics

This program is clearly not just about making projects.

What this program is really about

This program is not just only about building projects.

It's about:

  • how honestly you work
  • how well you document your process
  • how clearly you explain what you learned
  • how deeply you understand your circuit

We care more about:

  • your thinking
  • your learning
  • your effort

than just the final result.

What we look for

A simple project with a clear explanation is better than a complex project you don't understand.

We reward:

  • honest work
  • clear journals
  • real understanding
  • consistent effort

Eligibility

  • You must be 13-18 years old
  • You must be part of the Hack Club Slack community
  • You must not be banned from Hack Club
  • You must verify your identity

Project Requirements

  • Spend at least more than 1-2 hours per project
  • Build your designs using PCB Design tools
  • Write a short explanation of:
    • what you built
    • how it works
  • Avoid fully prebuilt hardware unless you understand them
  • Projects must be new work (no dipping)

Rules

  • Draw schematics
  • Explain your work in your own words
  • Submit to your GitHub repo
  • Spend at least 1-2 hours per project
  • Use safe devices and low voltage (12V)
