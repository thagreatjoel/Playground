Hardware Playground

break it learn it
Build circuits by messing with electronics, and open up real devices to see what’s actually going on inside.


---

What is this?

Most people use electronics every day  but don’t really know how they work.

Hardware Playground helps you fix that.

You will:

- open simple devices (like a mouse or remote)
- look at the circuit board (PCB)
- build your own circuits
- experiment and see what happens

You don’t need experience. You just need curiosity.

---

How it works (simple)

You follow this flow:

«open → understand → rebuild → experiment → explain»

You can go step-by-step or jump around.

---

What you can do

- open safe devices (mouse, toy, remote)
- find parts like resistors, LEDs, chips
- follow how electricity flows
- rebuild small parts on a breadboard
- change things and test results
- design your own circuits later

---

Build Options

🔧 Breadboard (best for learning)

- easy to use
- no soldering
- lets you experiment

👉 You get extra rewards for breadboard builds.

---

🧩 PCB Design (advanced)

- design your own circuit board
- apply for a grant to build it

---

💠 Reward System (simple)

You earn rewards in two steps:

1. 🔌 Earn Resistors

Resistors = base points

You get them based on:

- how good your project is
- what you wrote in your journal
- how much effort you put

👉 Better explanation = more points

---

2. 🔁 Convert to Diodes (💠)

You convert Resistors → 💠 Diodes

💠 Diodes are used to:

- get electronics kits
- get components
- unlock tools

---

🧠 How rewards are decided

- 70% → your journal (what you learned)
- 30% → time spent

Track time here:
👉 https://lapse.hackclub.com

---

🧠 Bonus rewards

You get extra 💠 if you:

- explain clearly
- show experiments
- show what changed and why

---

💰 PCB Grants

If your project is good, you can apply for a grant.

We can help pay for your PCB.

Grant levels:

- $10 → simple
- $25–50 → medium
- $50–100 → advanced

You must:

- explain your design
- build it
- show it working
